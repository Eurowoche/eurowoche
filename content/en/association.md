## About us

Our association bears the name Arbeitskreis Europäische Jugendwochen Burg Ludwigstein e.V. It is based in Witzenhausen and registered in the association register of the Eschwege district court. The association serves exclusively and directly charitable purposes.

## More than just dance and music: Communication at eye level

Culture comes alive with us. We believe that singing, making music and dancing together are the best ways to overcome language barriers and have genuine conversations. While the early meetings were primarily shaped by folk dance, choral and instrumental music have also become pillars of our shared work today.

**Diversity as standard:** From the very first hours, groups from all over Europe were involved – from Sweden to Austria.

**Bridges to Eastern Europe:** Early on, groups from Poland, Hungary and Bulgaria visited us. After the fall of the Iron Curtain, friends from the new German states, the Baltics and Ukraine joined.

**Openness for all:** We promote cooperation among European youth and stand as a sign for solidarity.

## Purpose of the Association

The association pursues exclusively charitable purposes, realized through: organization and implementation of the European Youth Weeks at Burg Ludwigstein, promotion of the European idea and intercultural exchange, support of participating groups, nurturing cooperation with European partner organizations, and strengthening understanding, friendship and solidarity across national, ethnic and religious borders.

## Die Ludwigsteiner

Die Ludwigsteiner are the association's own dance group. The dancers come together from all over Germany. For this reason, they only meet a few times a year, but then rehearse very intensively and with great passion.

The group showcases various German traditions, which they dance into stories. They use old folk dances and various traditional costumes, working them into new choreographies. They are always accompanied live by the music group "die Lusiker."

**Key facts:** Founded 1990 by Fritz Lenz. Since 1991 the permanent German representation at the European Youth Week. International performances in over 10 countries worldwide. Current leadership: Annika Meyer & Johanna Klauer.

## Die Ludwigsteiner Musiker

The Lusiker (a wordplay combining Ludwigsteiner Musiker) are the association's own music group. The 7-member team, together with the Ludwigsteiner dance group, represents Germany at the European Youth Week.

**Key facts:** Current leadership: Maciej Derra. Instruments: flute, accordion, bass, guitar, horn, vocals.

## Member of CIOFF®

The association is a member of CIOFF® (Conseil International des Organisations de Festivals de Folklore et d'Arts Traditionnels), an international partner organization of UNESCO in the field of traditional culture and folklore.
