The European Youth Week looks back on a long tradition of encounter, exchange and international understanding.

## Our History – A vision connecting generations

The European Youth Week is no coincidence – it is a response to history. It was founded in 1953 by Hella Heynmöller. Under the impression of World War II, she pursued a clear goal: the youth of Europe should get to know, respect and understand each other, so that peace would become a matter of course.

What began over 70 years ago with 60 participants is today a vibrant movement. To date, around 300 folklore groups with approximately 10,000 young people have shared this adventure.

## The Beginnings (1953)

So that something like this would never happen again, the youth of Europe should get to know, respect and understand each other. The founder chose the symbolic Burg Ludwigstein as the place of European encounter. It is located directly on the former inner-German border and was and remains a center of the German youth movement.

In 1953, the first meeting took place with 60 participants: a folk dance group from Austria, individual participants from Sweden, Denmark, Switzerland and West Germany.

## Music, Dance and Organization in Transition

From 1962-1984, Ward de Beer led the choral and orchestral work. In 1981, the Arbeitskreis Europäische Jugendwochen Burg Ludwigstein e.V. was founded.

## Financing and Markets

Since 1983, the association has organized an annual Easter egg market and since 1985 a Christmas market at Burg Ludwigstein. The proceeds serve to finance the European Youth Weeks.

## Internationalization & Die Ludwigsteiner

A major innovation was the founding of the association's own folklore group "Die Ludwigsteiner" by Fritz Lenz in autumn 1990. After the fall of the inner-German border, East German groups also joined from 1990.

## Jubilees and significance today

In 2006, the European Youth Week celebrated its 50th anniversary, followed by the 60th Eurowoche in 2018. In total, around 300 folklore groups with approximately 10,000 participants have taken part.
